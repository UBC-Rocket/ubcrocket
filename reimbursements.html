We have 2 types of accounts: PAF (Professional Activities Fund) accounts and a Main account. Only APSC has direct access to these accounts - to use the money in them, we gather the required documents (claim form, bank statement and receipt) and request a reimbursement from APSC. Reimbursements must be approved via email by the account signatories (project leads) and our faculty advisor.

Each individual project applies to UBC to receive money from PAF at the start of the academic year (around September), which is stored in its own PAF account. Money from PAF granted to a specific project can only be used by that project. Any PAF funding we don't use is returned to UBC at the end of the year (August).

The money in our Main account comes from our generous sponsors and donors, so we are not required to return it to anybody at any stage. We decide how we want to use it.

Because PAF disappears, we use it before the money in our Main account in every case. If you are requesting a reimbursement from a project's PAF account, complete that project's PAF reimbursement claim form in Step 1. Otherwise, complete the main account claim form. Steps 2 and 3 remain the same.
