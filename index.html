Welcome to UBC ROCKET

We are a student engineering design team at the University of British Columbia dedicated to the design, manufacture and launch of suborbital rockets. If you are looking for a fast-paced team with ambitious goals and are willing to get your hands dirty, this is the team for you.

01.

Design and build rockets

Currently we are in the process of designing and building a rocket from the ground up. The rocket is slated to fly at the 2017 Intercollegiate Rocket Engineering Competition held annually in Green River, Utah.

Designing a rocket is an enormous challenge and requires students of all engineering backgrounds to work together. Being part of UBC Rocket allows you to collect real world experience in a challenging and dynamic environment while working on some really exciting technology!

The team is currently working in the following areas.

  • Structure
  • Propulsion
  • Avionics
  • Payload
  • Recovery
  • Ground Support Equipment
  • Systems & Integration
  • Safety

02.

Develop and integrate payloads

As part of the Intercollegiate Rocket Engineering Competition, the team is developing a payload bay/adapter and a payload. We have been given a lot of freedom when it comes to the payload so we have the opportunity to be really creative here.

We are also interested in integrating external payloads. If your group/team is interested in hitching a ride on our rocket please let us know.

03.

Research future propulsion methods

In the first year of the competition, the team will be using a solid fuel rocket motor.

For future years, we are looking to innovate and a logical step to pursue is a more advanced propulsion system. ARocket propulsion is an extremely exciting, challenging and time-intensive field and as such this project will likely span several years.

Looking for a big challenge during your time at UBC? This team is for you.
